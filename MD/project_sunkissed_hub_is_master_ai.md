In Sunkissed Soul (Sunso), the placeholder labeled "hub AI" in the prototype IS Master AI. They are not two products — Sunkissed Soul is the personalization shell (character profiles, user profiles, knows-you-better-than-you-know-yourself layer), Master AI is the local hub brain it plugs into, and Pupils are the per-person browser interfaces that all share that one brain. New working components don't need new homes — they hang off the Master AI hub.

**Why:** Elijah said it 2026-04-24: "My whole thought process is — I just didn't know where to add more working components on my Sunso app, where you see 'hub AI', that would be Master AI." The reason this matters: it converts what looked like THREE separate projects (Master AI, Pupil, Sunkissed Soul) into ONE arc with three layers — hub, interface, personalization. The "digital relative by 2030" destination needs all three.

**How to apply:**
- When Elijah says "Sunso" or "Sunkissed Soul," the brain underneath is Master AI — don't propose a separate AI engine.
- New feature ideas that would seem to need "a new app" should be evaluated as: (a) hub-level (lives in Master AI), (b) interface-level (lives in Pupil), (c) personalization-level (lives in Sunkissed Soul shell). Pick a layer; don't sprawl.
- Character profiles + user profiles live at the Sunkissed layer, NOT in Master AI's flat memory file. Master AI's memory is shared/operational; Sunkissed profiles are per-person.
- Cross-references: `project_apps_built.md` (only two agents = Sensei + Pupil — Sunkissed is a *shell over* Sensei, not a third agent), `project_sunkissed_vision.md` (off-grid + flagship arc), `project_digital_relative_vision.md` (the destination this architecture serves).
- This is architectural memory — if Elijah ever rewords or reorganizes (e.g., decides Sunkissed IS the brand and Master AI is internal), update this file rather than fork.
