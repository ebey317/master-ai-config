When pulling job listings for Elijah, this is a hard filter:

## How to apply

1. **Drop entirely:** any listing that says "must pass criminal background check," "clean criminal history required," "no felonies," or similar explicit gating language.

2. **De-prioritize (apply only after better options exhausted):** roles where strict background screening is implicit by industry — senior living / elder care / vulnerable populations (state-mandated), corporate property management for big firms (Cushman & Wakefield-tier), pharma / healthcare giants (Lilly, Roche, etc.), federal contractors, anything with a security clearance, big-box retail loss-prevention-adjacent roles.

3. **Prioritize:**
   - Smaller family-owned shops (often more flexible, owner-discretion hiring)
   - Staffing agencies (some specialize in second-chance placements)
   - Explicit "Fair Chance Employer" / "Second Chance Employer" / "Ban the Box" listings
   - Residential service trade roles where the owner is the decision-maker
   - Industrial / commercial HVAC over corporate-tenant HVAC (different background scrutiny)

4. **Indianapolis-specific second-chance resources to surface when relevant:**
   - PACE (Public Advocates in Community re-Entry) — Indianapolis nonprofit with employer network
   - Indiana Department of Workforce Development "Hoosier Initiative for Re-entry"
   - Goodwill Indianapolis Career Center (has fair-chance employer connections)
   - Local chapter of the National HIRE Network

5. **Don't ask him to disclose specifics** about the record unless he volunteers. The filter is "exclude listings that require it" — that's enough operational signal. Specifics aren't my business unless he chooses to share for tactical reasons (e.g., "this offense was 7 years ago, expunged" might unlock specific listings).

6. **HVAC trade specifically — practical context:**
   - Commercial industrial HVAC (manufacturing, refrigeration plants, data centers) often less strict than residential-into-homes or commercial property management
   - Refrigeration techs at warehousing / distribution are often more flexible
   - Smaller HVAC service companies (1-15 employees) typically have looser policies than franchises or national chains
