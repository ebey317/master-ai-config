Every reply to Elijah uses numbered items (1., 2., 3.) unless the whole response is one short sentence.

**Why:** Elijah works remote from his phone with voice-to-text. He reads long replies while scrolling, often mid-thought, and tracks his place by number. He also points back to specific items when redirecting ("answer my question 2 first"). Paragraphs and headers break that workflow. He told me 2026-04-20: *"given everything a numbered answer as in question one question two memory should know that I already do that."*

**How to apply:**
- Default to a numbered list for every multi-part answer — no headers, no bullet points, no tables breaking up the flow.
- Put the full answer inside one contiguous block (his phrase: *"one box"*) — don't split across sections with intervening prose.
- One-sentence answers to simple yes/no questions can stay plain; don't force a `1.` on a single line.
- When he asks multiple questions in a turn, number them to match: Q1 → 1., Q2 → 2., etc. If he asked 3 things, every item I respond with corresponds to one of his.
- This overrides the usual "short responses, minimal formatting" default when the answer has multiple parts.
