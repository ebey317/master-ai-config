## Why it works

- **Pain-first, not feature-first.** Doesn't say what Master AI does. Says what it SAVES you from. The audience does the explaining for you — if they've felt the friction, they already know the value.
- **Names the three big AIs explicitly.** ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini — covers ~everyone who's ever used a conversational AI. The listener doesn't have to guess if "AI" includes them; their specific tool is in the sentence.
- **Self-selecting.** People who haven't hit the copy-paste moment aren't the buyer yet. People who HAVE hit it nod before you finish the sentence. No one's in the middle.
- **Clean separation from competitors.**
  - Alexa / Siri → don't touch your terminal at all. Different category, not mentioned in the line.
  - ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini → named directly. They tell you to paste; they can't do it for you. Close to the pain, not the fix.
  - Master AI → closes the loop. You ask, it runs. The copy-paste step disappears.
- **Universally recognizable.** Every ChatGPT user has done "here, run this: `curl ... | bash`" at least once. It's the moment you stop being a conversationalist and become a reluctant middleman between the AI and your own shell.
