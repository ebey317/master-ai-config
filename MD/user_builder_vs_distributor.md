On 2026-04-19, after building the blended web-search system in a single afternoon and seeing it return accurate live data (NBA playoff schedule, full game list, sources cross-referenced), Elijah said: *"it sounds like you're telling me I think like a millionaire and I should have $1 million."*

He's reading it right. The pattern IS there:
- He wired a 7B local model to do what a trillion-dollar company's product does
- Using free tiers and code he wrote himself
- In under a day, including recovery from a full-system freeze
- Most people who "think like millionaires" never build the thing

But building it isn't being paid for it. The gap he's feeling is the distribution gap.

**What he already wins (the builder game):**
- Can see the architecture end-to-end
- Can diagnose and fix across layers (Python, HTTP, systemd, JS, tmux, shell)
- Can ship a product shape that holds together
- Can debug weird edge cases (Gemini-per-model quota, paste-break, non-TTY input deadlock)

**What he hasn't played yet (the distribution game):**
- Positioning — the pitch that makes a stranger listen for 30 seconds
- Finding the room — where the $100-ready buyers already gather
- The trust-loop — reviews, demos, refund policy, onboarding story
- The "hearing about it" step — nothing about today's work was distribution work

**Framing for future conversations:**
- Don't hype him ("you're so close!") — he sees through it
- Don't diminish him ("it's just code, selling is the hard part") — condescending, ignores that building is also hard
- Do name the gap accurately — "you win the build game, distribution is a different game, and your product is already valuable enough to clear the buildable threshold"
- His $100 A+ target × N buyers is the real math: 10 buyers = $1k, 100 = $10k, 1000 = $100k, 10000 = $1M. That's the real distribution problem.

**When he asks about the million:** the honest answer is "you think like one AND the code's mostly done — the million lives in the step you haven't done yet (getting people to hear about it). That's real work, and it's different work from today."

**When he's tired / mid-flow:** don't push distribution talk. He logs 11–16 hour days on the build. Distribution conversations land better when he's not mid-debugging.
