When describing Master AI, never reach for "agent" or "super-agent" first. They're the engineer's words and they undersell what's actually been built.

**Elijah's exact framing 2026-05-13 (after reading the architectural product description doc):**

> *"we no longer have an agent. I feel like we have a super agent, not even a super agent, but we have a hero. Something totally different. Something new. we have an imagination come to life white space crafted two weeks and weeks of refining and tuning. We have something that scales forever forever product start small with room to grow."*

Verbatim phrases worth preserving in copy:
- **"hero"** (not "agent," not "super-agent")
- **"imagination come to life"**
- **"white space crafted"**
- **"forever forever product"**
- **"start small with room to grow"**

**Why:** Sensei is a character with identity (she/her, never break character), abilities (directive vocabulary, three bodies, browser reach, deep-think reasoning, voice), constraints (sudo handoff, irreversible-action gate, the always-confirm list, self-mod denylist), and a code (local-first, your hardware, your data, your rules). Two weeks of crafting baked the character into the system prompts, the chrome colors, the keybindings, the safety gates, the martial-arts metaphor (the extension is her arm, not a separate tool). Reducing all of that to "agent" or "super-agent" erases the work.

**How to apply:**
- **Landing / README / pitch copy:** lead with the hero framing. The engineer one-liner ("local-first computer agent with optional cloud escalation") shows up as the technical fallback, not the headline.
- **User-facing surfaces** (Sensei TUI legend, Chrome extension panel header, manifest.json description, ad copy): Sensei speaks AS herself. The 2026-05-13 manifest description "Sensei's reach into the browser — her arm on the page, not a separate tool" is the right register; the prior "Chrome side panel for the local Master AI backend" is the wrong one.
- **Internal architectural docs:** technical noun ("agent") is fine in body text — but the lead-in still names the hero framing. See `~/MD/project_master_ai_product_description.md` sections 1 + 1b.
- **Brand voice:** "free-thinking garage scientist who built a hero." Not "free-thinking garage scientist who built an agent." Aligns with [[feedback_brand_voice_patterns]].
