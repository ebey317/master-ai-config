**Elijah's words (verbatim, 2026-04-19):**

> "Now as long as you got your chat files and everything saved appropriately, this AI officially knows you and it's gonna move as fast as it can to help you. You basically can work hands-free all day just telling it to do stuff and it runs commands, changes the system, builds apps, helps you study. It's basically having a genius right next to you."

## Why this is the pitch

Everything about this line is on-message:

- **"knows you"** — cuts straight to the memory/context wedge (what cloud AIs can't do because they're multi-tenant)
- **"move as fast as it can"** — acknowledges local-model honesty (not pretending to match GPT-4's speed, celebrating that it tries its best for YOU specifically)
- **"work hands-free all day"** — the agentic-Sensei + voice + TTS + long-running session reality
- **"telling it to do stuff"** — matches Elijah's "programming the computer, not using it" insight; you give instructions, it executes
- **"runs commands, changes the system, builds apps, helps you study"** — concrete use cases, four of them, touching every surface (Sensei for commands + system, Sensei + Pupil for apps, learn.sh + Pupil for study)
- **"genius right next to you"** — humanizes it. Not a tool, a peer. This is the emotional hook.

## How to use this copy

1. **`slideshow.html`** — final slide tagline (replaces current "Welcome to the dojo" close)

3. **Gumroad / marketing site** — the product tagline under the title
4. **Installer final screen** — after "INSTALL COMPLETE," before the menu opens

### Derivative copy this unlocks

- **Headline:** "Your own AI. It knows you. It works next to you."
- **Subhead:** "Runs commands. Changes settings. Builds apps. Helps you study. All hands-free. All yours."
- **3-bullet pitch:**
  - *Genius-level capability* on your own hardware
  - *Knows you* — persistent memory, your files, your projects
  - *Works next to you* — agentic, hands-free, voice-optional
- **One-liner test** (does a prospect "get it"?): *"Imagine a genius sitting next to you who's already read all your notes, runs commands for you, and never leaves."*

## How to apply

- When writing any buyer-facing copy, lean on this line. Don't invent new ways to say what it already says.
- When tempted to describe features in isolation, frame them as "what your genius neighbor would do for you" instead.
